The Nintendo 64, also known as the N64, was a video game console released in 1964, hence the name N64. The console was renouned for its innovative use of 3D graphics, being the most powerful video game console on the market when it was released. The N64 was coded using C, which put off many developers, meaning that only 10 games were ever released for it. In 1965, due to death of the mascot of the console, Mario Mario, the console was permanently discontinued. However, for it's short availability, it offered a small selection of incredibly interesting games.

Super Luigi 16

The first game released on the system was Super Luigi 16, the 16th installment in the hit franchise. The game was a huge success, selling over 17 copies worldwide. The game was one of the first 3D platformers to be released, and even through poor camera controls, was critically adored.

There were initially plans to create a sequel to Super Luigi 16, with the development title being Super Luigi 20, taking after both Apple and Microsoft in skipping numbers in franchises. The director of the game, Shiguru Miyamoto, spoke out in disappointment when the game was cancelled, saying that it would have been the greatest in the series up to that point.

However, the cancellation of the console saw the game, and the series as a whole, being put on hold indefinitely. Supposedly leaked versions of this Super Luigi 20's rom have been uploaded to various sites throughout the years, but their authenticity is currently unknown.
